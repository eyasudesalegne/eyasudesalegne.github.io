A question behind my work

The body speaks in signals. I build tools that listen carefully.

How can we turn complex biological signals into evidence that researchers and clinicians can actually trust?

I’m Eyasu Desalegne Beyene. I’m a biomedical engineer and PhD candidate working from retinal images and neural dynamics to computational microscopy—where trustworthy AI meets practical engineering.

01 — About

Engineering at the intersection of intelligence, imaging, and human health.

My research asks how advanced biomedical technologies can become more reliable, explainable, and useful in real experimental settings—especially where resources are limited.

I work across medical-image analysis, neural systems modeling, computational microscopy, and biomedical instrumentation. Across these areas, I care about calibrated evidence, leakage-controlled evaluation, documented assumptions, and systems that other researchers can reproduce and improve.
