About

A research profile built between engineering, imaging, and systems thinking.

My work is shaped by a practical question: how can advanced biomedical technologies become more reproducible, explainable, and accessible in real research environments? I approach this through medical imaging AI, computational microscopy, neural simulation software, and biomedical instrumentation.

I am especially interested in systems that are not only technically impressive, but also inspectable: pipelines that can be tested, repeated, explained, and improved by other researchers.

Research principles

How I want the work to be judged

These principles keep the portfolio professional and trustworthy: no exaggerated claims, no opaque pipelines, and no separation between theory and implementation.

Reproducible by design

Projects should include clear configuration, versioned code, documented assumptions, and repeatable experiments wherever possible.

Clinically cautious

Medical AI should be evaluated with calibration, robustness, failure modes, and honest status labels rather than only headline accuracy.

Accessible engineering

Biomedical systems matter more when they can be built, understood, repaired, and adapted in constrained research settings.
